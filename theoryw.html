Wavelength of laser Light

Theory
  • It is generally assumed that light travels in a straight line but it suffers some deviation from its straight path in passing close to edges of opaque obstacles and narrow slits.
  • Some of the light does bend into the region of geometrical shadow and its intensity falls off rapidly.
  • This bending of light which is not due to reflection/refraction is called as DIFFRACTION
